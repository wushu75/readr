Legal · Privacy Policy

We collect
almost nothing.

READR is built on a simple belief: your reading habits are yours. We designed the extension to work entirely on your device, without needing to know who you are or what you read.

Last updated: February 2026 · Effective immediately

TL;DR — Plain English Summary
  • READR runs entirely in your browser. Your articles never leave your device.
  • We don't track what you read, when you read it, or how long you spend reading.
  • Settings (theme, font size, etc.) are stored locally via Chrome's sync storage — controlled by you, not us.
  • The only time we see your data: when you pay for Pro (Stripe handles it) or email us for support.
  • We don't sell data. We don't run ads. We make money from the $19.99 one-time purchase.
01

Who we are

READR is a Chrome browser extension that provides a distraction-free reading view for web articles. It is operated by READR ("we," "us," or "our"). If you have questions about this policy, you can reach us at privacy@readr.uk.

This Privacy Policy applies to the READR Chrome extension, the READR website at readr.app, and any related services we operate.

03

Chrome storage — how your settings sync

READR uses the Chrome storage.sync API to remember your preferences (theme, font size, line width, auto-open domains, and Pro status). This is a built-in Chrome feature:

  • If you're signed into Chrome, Google syncs this data across your devices — subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • We never have access to this data. It lives in your Chrome profile.
  • You can clear it at any time: chrome://settings/clearBrowserData or by removing the extension.
04

Pro purchases and license keys

When you purchase READR Pro, payment is processed by Stripe, Inc. Stripe is a certified PCI-DSS Level 1 service provider. We never receive or store your full card number, CVV, or any raw payment credentials.

What we do store on our servers:

  • Your email address — used to send your license key and for support inquiries.
  • Your license key — a randomly generated token tied to your purchase, used to verify Pro status.
  • The Stripe session ID — for reconciliation and fraud prevention.

We retain purchase records for up to 7 years for accounting and legal compliance. You can request deletion of your email at any time; we will anonymise your record while retaining the transaction ID for accounting purposes.

Stripe's privacy policy: stripe.com/privacy

05

The website (readr.app)

The READR website may collect basic server logs (IP address, browser type, page requested, timestamp) through our hosting provider. These are used only for security and uptime monitoring and are not linked to your identity or reading habits.

We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any other third-party tracking or advertising scripts on readr.app.

06

Permissions we request — and why

The Chrome Web Store requires us to disclose why each permission is used. Here's a plain-English explanation:

  • activeTab — Needed to activate the reading overlay on the page you're currently viewing. We only access the tab when you click the READR icon.
  • scripting — Needed to inject the reading overlay and Readability parser into the page. No scripts are injected on pages you haven't asked READR to open on.
  • storage — Needed to save your settings (theme, font, etc.) locally via Chrome's storage API.
  • tabs — Needed to open the upgrade page in a new tab when you click "Upgrade to Pro."

We do not request broad host permissions. We do not read your browsing history.

07

Third-party services

READR uses a small number of third-party services, listed here in full:

  • Stripe — payment processing for Pro purchases. stripe.com/privacy
  • Google Fonts — typography on the website only (not in the extension). Subject to Google's Privacy Policy. The extension itself uses system fonts.
  • Mozilla Readability.js — an open-source content-extraction library that runs entirely locally in your browser. It makes no network requests. Source code.

We do not use any advertising networks, data brokers, or analytics platforms.

08

Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights including the right to access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you. Because we collect so little, there usually isn't much to request — but we honour all lawful requests promptly.

  • Access — you can ask what data we hold about you (typically: your email and license key).
  • Deletion — you can ask us to delete your email. We will do so and anonymise your purchase record.
  • Correction — if your email changed, let us know and we'll update it.
  • Portability — we can provide a copy of any data we hold in a machine-readable format.
  • Opt-out of sale — we don't sell data. There's nothing to opt out of.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@readr.uk. We'll respond within 30 days.

09

Children's privacy

READR is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.
